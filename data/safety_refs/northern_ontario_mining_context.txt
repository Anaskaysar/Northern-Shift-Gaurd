--- NORTHERN ONTARIO MINING CONTEXT ---

Northern Ontario (the "Northern Shield" region including Sudbury, Timmins, Thunder Bay, Kirkland Lake) is home to some of Canada's most significant mining operations:
- Nickel, copper, zinc, gold, and platinum group metals
- Major operators: Vale, Glencore, Barrick, Newmont, Kinross
- Mix of underground hard rock and open pit surface operations
- Workforce: aging — significant retirement wave underway, creating experience gaps

--- KEY SAFETY CHALLENGES ---

1. New Worker Risk
- Northern Ontario mines are onboarding new workers at high rates to replace retirees
- New workers have significantly higher injury rates in the first 6 months
- PPE compliance is highest-priority for new workers who may not have formed habits

2. Shift Rotation and Fatigue
- Many operations run 12-hour rotating shifts (days/nights)
- 4-day on / 4-day off or 7-on / 7-off schedules common
- Night shift workers and workers transitioning from day to night have highest fatigue risk
- Remote fly-in / fly-out camps increase fatigue accumulation

3. Mobile Equipment Hazards
- Mobile equipment (haul trucks, LHDs, scooptrams, jumbo drills) is the leading cause of fatalities
- Hi-vis vest non-compliance near mobile equipment is the most common critical PPE violation
- Pedestrian-vehicle interaction is the #1 fatality risk on surface mine sites

4. Underground vs Surface PPE
- Underground: hard hat mandatory, lamp on hard hat, self-rescuer device
- Surface: hard hat + hi-vis in active zones; fire-resistant coveralls near processing
- Both: safety footwear, eye protection when required by task

--- WSIB (Workplace Safety and Insurance Board) REQUIREMENTS ---

Ontario employers must:
- Document all PPE non-compliance incidents
- Provide PPE training at hire and annually
- Maintain inspection records for PPE
- Report lost-time injuries related to PPE failure within 3 days

WSIB mining sector injury rates (2022 data):
- Underground metal mining: 2.8 injuries per 100 workers (above provincial average)
- Surface metal mining: 1.9 injuries per 100 workers
- Most common injury types: slips/trips/falls (34%), struck by object (22%), overexertion (18%)

--- NORTHERN SHIFT CONTEXT FOR AI SCREENING ---

For AI-based shift-start screening in Northern Ontario:
- Priority 1: Hard hat + hi-vis vest (regulatory, stop-work if missing)
- Priority 2: Visible fatigue cues (supervisor judgment, not medical — but critical for mobile equipment operators)
- Priority 3: Other PPE (task-specific — eye, foot, hand protection checked by task assignment)

Shift supervisor decision framework:
- STOP WORK: Missing hard hat or hi-vis in active zone (Ontario Reg 854 s.79, s.81)
- INTERVENE: PPE unclear or high fatigue — verify before floor access
- MONITOR: Moderate fatigue cues — increased check-ins, no solo equipment operation
- CLEAR: All PPE present, no fatigue indicators — proceed with normal shift
