URL: https://www.osha.gov/mining
Jurisdiction: United States Federal (reference standard for Ontario operations)

--- HEAD PROTECTION (29 CFR 1910.135 / MSHA 30 CFR 56.15001) ---

Hard hats are required:
- In all areas where there is a potential for objects to fall from above
- Near overhead work, hoisting operations, drilling, blasting
- In underground mine entries and active stopes
- Around mobile equipment and conveyor systems

Hard hat standards: ANSI/ISEA Z89.1 (US) or CSA Z94.1 (Canada)
- Class E (electrical): for areas with electrical hazards
- Class G (general): standard mine site requirement
- Hard hats must be inspected before each shift; retire if cracked, dented, or showing UV degradation

--- HIGH-VISIBILITY / REFLECTIVE APPAREL (MSHA 30 CFR 56.14132) ---

High-visibility clothing required for:
- All workers in areas where mobile equipment operates
- Surface mine haulage roads, active open pits, processing plants
- Any area where line-of-sight with equipment operators cannot be guaranteed

Standard: ANSI/ISEA 107 Class 2 or 3
- Fluorescent yellow-green, orange-red, or red
- Retroreflective tape in X or H pattern on torso and shoulders
- Vest, jacket, or coverall — torso coverage required

--- EYE PROTECTION ---

Required when: drilling, blasting, grinding, welding, exposure to chemicals or flying particles
Standard: ANSI Z87.1

--- FATIGUE AND IMPAIRMENT ---

MSHA 30 CFR 56.20005 — Physical and Mental Condition
"Persons shall not perform work if their physical or mental condition is such as to endanger themselves or others."

MSHA guidance on fatigue in mining:
- Extended shifts and rotating schedules are primary fatigue contributors
- Supervisors are responsible for assessing worker fitness at shift start
- Workers should not operate haul trucks, LHDs, or other mobile equipment if showing fatigue signs
- Fatigue is a leading contributing factor in mining equipment accidents

Signs of fatigue to watch for:
- Difficulty keeping eyes open or frequent eye-rubbing
- Head nodding or drooping
- Delayed responses to questions
- Reduced coordination or slowed movement
- Irritability or mood changes

--- PENALTY STRUCTURE ---

MSHA significant-and-substantial (S&S) violations for PPE non-compliance:
- First violation: $250–$5,000
- Unwarrantable failure: up to $70,000 per violation
- Pattern of violations: potential mine closure order

--- SUPERVISOR QUICK REFERENCE ---

STOP WORK triggers:
- Worker entering active area without hard hat
- Worker near mobile equipment without hi-vis vest

MONITOR / INTERVENE triggers:
- Worker showing fatigue cues before equipment operation
- Damaged or non-compliant PPE (worn but inadequate)

DOCUMENT:
- All PPE non-compliance incidents must be logged in the shift record
- Fatigue assessments should note observable indicators, not diagnose medically
