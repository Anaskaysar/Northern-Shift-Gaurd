Jurisdiction: Ontario, Canada

--- PERSONAL PROTECTIVE EQUIPMENT ---

Section 79 — High-Visibility Apparel
(1) An employer shall ensure that a worker who is exposed to the hazard of being struck by a vehicle or moving equipment wears a garment that:
  (a) covers at least the upper body
  (b) is fluorescent blaze orange, fluorescent yellow-green, or fluorescent red in colour
  (c) has retro-reflective material in a pattern that encircles the torso

Section 81 — Head Protection
(1) An employer shall ensure that a worker in an area where there is a risk of head injury wears head protection that:
  (a) is a safety helmet (hard hat) meeting CSA Standard Z94.1
  (b) is properly fitted and worn correctly
(2) No worker shall enter an underground mine, open pit, or active work zone without approved head protection.
(3) Hard hats must be replaced if cracked, damaged, or past the manufacturer's service life.

Section 83 — Eye and Face Protection
Workers shall wear appropriate eye protection when exposed to:
  - Flying particles, dust, or debris
  - Hazardous chemical splashes
  - Welding or cutting operations

Section 85 — Foot Protection
Workers in areas where there is a risk of foot injury shall wear CSA-approved safety footwear with:
  - Steel or composite toe caps
  - Puncture-resistant soles where applicable

--- FATIGUE MANAGEMENT ---

Section 32 — Fitness for Work
(1) No worker shall work in a mine or mining plant if the worker's ability to perform work safely is impaired by fatigue, illness, or other cause.
(2) A supervisor who becomes aware that a worker may be impaired by fatigue shall:
  (a) remove the worker from safety-critical duties
  (b) ensure the worker is assessed before reassignment to equipment operation

Section 10 — Supervisor Responsibilities
A supervisor shall ensure that:
  (a) the measures and procedures prescribed by this Regulation are carried out in the workplace
  (b) every worker under the supervisor's authority works in compliance with this Regulation
  (c) workers showing signs of fatigue or impairment are not assigned to operate mobile equipment or work in areas with moving machinery

--- ENFORCEMENT ---

Violations of Ontario Regulation 854 can result in:
  - Stop-work orders issued by a Ministry of Labour inspector
  - Fines up to $500,000 per offence (individuals) or $2,000,000 (corporations) under the Occupational Health and Safety Act
  - Director's orders requiring corrective action

--- PRACTICAL SUMMARY FOR SHIFT SUPERVISORS ---

Before every shift, supervisors must confirm:
1. HARD HAT: Every worker entering an active area must have a properly fitted, undamaged hard hat. Missing or damaged hard hat = stop-work, do not allow entry.
2. HI-VIS VEST: Any worker near mobile equipment or in active zones must wear a fluorescent high-visibility vest or jacket. Missing vest = do not allow floor access until corrected.
3. FATIGUE: Workers showing visible fatigue signs (difficulty keeping eyes open, slurred speech, slowed reactions, head drooping) must be assessed. Do not assign to equipment operation. This is a supervisor judgment call — not a medical diagnosis.
